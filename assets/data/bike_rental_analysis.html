# Count how many rentals occur on each day of the week
day_counts = df["Day of Week"].value_counts()

# Import matplotlib for plotting
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Plot a bar chart showing rentals per day
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
day_counts.plot(kind="bar")
plt.title("Bike Rentals by Day of the Week")
plt.xlabel("Day of the Week")
plt.ylabel("Number of Rentals")
plt.xticks(rotation=45)
plt.show()

This chart shows the distribution of bike rentals across the days of the week. According to the data, Thursday has the highest rental count, followed closely by the weekend and other weekdays. This suggests that bike rentals are not limited to leisure days but may also be popular during the weekpossibly for commuting or weekday events. The fairly consistent rental levels across most days indicate steady demand, with a slight peak mid-week. The company can use this insight to ensure sufficient bike availability throughout the week, particularly on Thursdays when demand is highest.

# Create a scatter plot to explore the relationship between temperature and rentals
x = df["Temperature"]         # X-axis: Temperature column
y = df["RENTALS"]             # Y-axis: Rentals column

# Plot scatter plot using matplotlib
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
plt.scatter(x, y, alpha=0.5)  # Use alpha for point transparency
plt.title("Temperature vs Bike Rentals")
plt.xlabel("Temperature (C)")
plt.ylabel("Number of Rentals")
plt.show()

This scatter plot shows the relationship between outdoor temperature and bike rental volume. We can observe that rentals are lower when temperatures are very cold, and they increase as temperatures rise to a comfortable range. However, extremely high temperatures do not show much additional increase in rentals, suggesting a comfort threshold. This pattern indicates that moderate temperatures encourage more rentals, while extreme cold discourages them. The company should consider increasing bike availability on days with mild, pleasant weather to meet higher expected demand.

# Group the data by season and calculate total rentals in each season
season_rentals = df.groupby("Season")["RENTALS"].sum()

# Import matplotlib for plotting
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Plot the totals as a horizontal bar chart
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 5))
season_rentals.plot(kind='barh', color='skyblue')
plt.title("Total Rentals by Season")
plt.xlabel("Total Number of Rentals")
plt.ylabel("Season")
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

This horizontal bar chart shows total bike rentals for each season. The data reveals that Summer has the highest number of rentals, followed by Autumn. Winter sees the lowest rentals, suggesting that cold weather significantly reduces bike usage. These trends help the company understand how rental demand shifts with the seasons. As a result, inventory, staff planning, and marketing strategies can be adjusted to focus more resources during peak seasons like Summer and Autumn.

Analysis Questions

1. In your analytical opinion, why are the majority of users renting bicycles?
The data suggests that most users rent bicycles for both commuting and leisure purposes. The highest rental activity occurs on Thursdays, a weekday, which indicates a strong commuting trend. However, steady weekend demand implies a secondary purpose likely recreational use. Bicycles offer a flexible and affordable mode of transportation in urban settings. This dual-purpose usage means the company serves both routine commuters and casual weekend riders.

2. Are there ideal weather conditions that create high bike rental demand? If so, what are they?
Temperature plays a significant role in rental demand. As shown in the scatter plot, bike rentals increase with warmer, moderate temperatures and drop off when it becomes too cold. There is no major increase during extreme heat, indicating a comfort range for users. These findings show that mild, dry, and pleasant weather drives the most activity. This can help the company prepare for demand surges based on forecast conditions.

3. How should the company strategize based on your results? In other words, what actionable steps can the company take in order to take advantage of your insights.
Based on the data, the company should prioritize having more bikes available during Summer and Autumn and on peak weekdays like Thursday. Promotional offers can be used in Winter or on lower-demand days to encourage usage. Weather-based planning and communication can also help manage customer expectations and improve experience. Segmented marketing one for commuters, one for weekend leisure users could also drive engagement. These strategies align resource planning with actual demand, leading to better service and more efficient operations.

Conclusion

This project revealed key patterns in how customers use the bike rental service. Demand peaks on Thursdays, indicating that weekday commuting plays a major role. Rentals are also heavily influenced by temperature, with moderate weather creating ideal conditions for riding. Summer and Autumn show the highest seasonal usage, while Winter sees the lowest demand. These findings highlight how both time-based and environmental factors impact usage. With this knowledge, the company can plan bike availability, staff scheduling, and promotional efforts more effectively. Overall, data-driven decision-making can help improve customer satisfaction, operational efficiency, and business performance.
